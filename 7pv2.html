🚫 The Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse

To make a marriage work, you must avoid the "Four Horsemen"—communication styles that predict relationship failure. Here is a summary with specific examples.

🗡️ Criticism

Attacking your partner's core character or personality, rather than complaining about a specific behavior.

Example: "You're such a liar!" or "You never think about how your behavior affects other people. You are so selfish."

Antidote: Use a gentle start-up. Say "I feel..." about a specific situation.

🙄 Contempt

Assuming a position of moral superiority over your partner. Includes sarcasm, cynicism, eye-rolling, and name-calling.

Example: "Oh, please. You couldn't balance a checkbook if your life depended on it. You're an idiot."

Antidote: Build a culture of appreciation and respect.

🛡️ Defensiveness

Playing the victim to ward off a perceived attack. Making excuses so that your partner will back off.

Example: "It's not my fault we're late! You never told me what time we were supposed to leave in the first place!"

Antidote: Take responsibility, even if only for part of the conflict.

🧱 Stonewalling

Withdrawing from the interaction, shutting down, and simply stopping responding to your partner.

Example: (Giving the silent treatment, physically turning away, or crossing arms and refusing to speak during an argument).

Antidote: Practice physiological self-soothing (take a 20-minute break).

💡 The 7 Principles Flashcards

Click on the flashcards below to flip them and learn about each of the 7 Principles for making marriage work.

1. Share Love Maps

Tap to flip ↺
The Concept:
A "Love Map" is the part of your brain where you store all the relevant information about your partner's life.

Action:
Know their worries, hopes, goals, and history. Continually update this map as they grow and change.

2. Nurture Fondness & Admiration

Tap to flip ↺
The Concept:
The antidote to contempt. It's the belief that your partner is worthy of honor and respect.

Action:
Remind yourself of your partner's positive qualities and verbally express your affection and appreciation out loud.

3. Turn Toward Each Other

Tap to flip ↺
The Concept:
Partners constantly make "bids" for attention, affection, or support. You can turn toward, turn away, or turn against them.

Action:
Engage with your partner's bids. Even a simple "That's interesting" builds the emotional bank account.

4. Let Your Partner Influence You

Tap to flip ↺
The Concept:
Marriages succeed when partners can share power and decision-making.

Action:
Be open to compromise. Don't dig your heels in; actively search for common ground and honor your partner's opinions and feelings.

5. Solve Solvable Problems

Tap to flip ↺
The Concept:
Not all problems are perpetual. Solvable problems have a solution.

Action:
1. Soften your startup.
2. Learn to make and receive repair attempts.
3. Soothe yourself and each other.
4. Compromise.
5. Be tolerant of each other's faults.

6. Overcome Gridlock

Tap to flip ↺
The Concept:
Gridlock happens when you have perpetual problems based on unfulfilled dreams or fundamental differences.

Action:
The goal isn't to solve the problem, but to move from gridlock to dialogue. Uncover the hidden dreams behind the conflict.

7. Create Shared Meaning

Tap to flip ↺
The Concept (Build Future Together):
Marriage isn't just about avoiding conflict; it's about building a rich, meaningful life together.

Action:
Create shared rituals, roles, goals, and symbols. Support each other's life dreams and create a family culture.
