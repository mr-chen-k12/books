Lesson Practice

Diagram this statement. Then answer the questions that follow.
Three eighths of the 40 little engines could climb the hill.
a. Into how many parts was the group divided?

The denominator of the fraction $\frac{3}{8}$ tells us how many parts the whole is divided into.

Answer: 8 parts
b. How many engines were in each part?

Divide the total number of engines (40) by the number of parts (8):

$$40 \div 8 = 5$$
Answer: 5 engines
c. How many parts could climb the hill?

The numerator of the fraction $\frac{3}{8}$ tells us how many parts are counted.

Answer: 3 parts
d. How many engines could climb the hill?

Multiply the number of parts (3) by the number of engines in each part (5):

$$3 \times 5 = 15$$
Answer: 15 engines
e. How many parts could not climb the hill?

Subtract the climbing parts from the total parts:

$$8 - 3 = 5$$
Answer: 5 parts
f. How many engines could not climb the hill?

Multiply the remaining parts (5) by the engines per part (5):

$$5 \times 5 = 25$$
Answer: 25 engines
Read the statement and then answer the questions that follow.
The face of a spinner is divided into 12 equal sectors. The probability of spinning red on one spin is $\frac{1}{4}$.
g. How many sectors are red?

Calculate $\frac{1}{4}$ of the total sectors (12):

$$\frac{1}{4} \times 12 = 3$$
Answer: 3 sectors
h. How many sectors are not red?

Subtract the red sectors from the total:

$$12 - 3 = 9$$
Answer: 9 sectors
i. What is the probability of spinning not red in one spin?

The probability is the ratio of "not red" sectors to total sectors:

$$P(\text{not red}) = \frac{9}{12}$$

Reduce the fraction by dividing numerator and denominator by 3:

$$\frac{3}{4}$$
Answer: $\frac{3}{4}$
j. What is the sum of the probabilities of spinning red and not red? How are the events related?

Add the two probabilities:

$$\frac{1}{4} + \frac{3}{4} = \frac{4}{4} = 1$$

Because the sum is 1 and they cover all possibilities, the events are complementary.

Answer: 1; The events are complementary.

Written Practice

1. The weight of an object on the Moon is about $\frac{1}{6}$ of its weight on Earth. A person weighing 114 pounds on Earth would weigh about how much on the Moon?

To find the weight on the Moon, divide the Earth weight by 6:

$$114 \div 6 = 19$$
Answer: 19 pounds

b. What percent of the times at bat did Mekhi get a hit?

$$\frac{6}{24}$$

a. Into how many parts is the class divided?
b. How many students are in each part?

d. How many girls are in the class?

a. Parts: The denominator is 5.

b. Students per part: $30 \div 5 = 6$ students.

c. Boys: The numerator is 3. So, $3 \text{ parts} \times 6 = 18$ boys.

d. Girls: Total students - Boys = $30 - 18 = 12$ girls.

9. EXPLAIN If the chance of rain is 60%, is it more likely to rain or not to rain? Why?

The chance of rain is 60%. The chance of it not raining is $100\% - 60\% = 40\%$.

Since 60% is greater than 40% (and greater than half, 50%), it is more likely to rain.

Answer: It is more likely to rain.
10. Three fifths of a circle is what percent of a circle?

Convert $\frac{3}{5}$ to a percent.

$$\frac{3}{5} = \frac{3 \times 20}{5 \times 20} = \frac{60}{100} = 60\%$$
Answer: 60%

14. $3 \frac{1}{5} - 1 \frac{3}{5}$

You cannot subtract $\frac{3}{5}$ from $\frac{1}{5}$. Borrow 1 from the 3.

$$3 \frac{1}{5} = 2 + 1 + \frac{1}{5} = 2 + \frac{5}{5} + \frac{1}{5} = 2 \frac{6}{5}$$

Now subtract:

$$2 \frac{6}{5} - 1 \frac{3}{5} = 1 \frac{3}{5}$$
Answer: $1 \frac{3}{5}$

Convert to mixed number:

$$1 \frac{7}{8}$$
Answer: $1 \frac{7}{8}$

21. Write the prime factorization of 1000 using exponents.

We know that $1000 = 10 \times 10 \times 10 = 10^3$.

The prime factorization of 10 is $2 \times 5$.

So, $1000 = (2 \times 5) \times (2 \times 5) \times (2 \times 5) = 2^3 \times 5^3$.

Answer: $2^3 \times 5^3$

29. Write the prime factorization of both the numerator and the denominator of this fraction. Then reduce the fraction.
$$ \frac{210}{350} $$

Numerator (210): $21 \times 10 = (3 \times 7) \times (2 \times 5) = 2 \times 3 \times 5 \times 7$.

Denominator (350): $35 \times 10 = (5 \times 7) \times (2 \times 5) = 2 \times 5 \times 5 \times 7$.

Reduce:

$$ \frac{2 \times 3 \times 5 \times 7}{2 \times 5 \times 5 \times 7} $$

Cancel common factors (2, 5, 7):

$$ \frac{\not{2} \times 3 \times \not{5} \times \not{7}}{\not{2} \times 5 \times \not{5} \times \not{7}} = \frac{3}{5} $$
Answer: $\frac{3}{5}$

Real-World Connection

Simplify each prime factorization below. Then identify which prime factorization does not belong in the group. Explain your reasoning.
$3^2 \times 2^3$    $2^2 \times 3^3 \times 5$    $2^2 \times 3^2 \times 5^2$    $3^5$    $2^4 \times 7^2$

Simplify:

  • $3^2 \times 2^3 = 9 \times 8 = 72$
  • $2^2 \times 3^3 \times 5 = 4 \times 27 \times 5 = 540$
  • $2^2 \times 3^2 \times 5^2 = 4 \times 9 \times 25 = 900$
  • $3^5 = 243$
  • $2^4 \times 7^2 = 16 \times 49 = 784$

Reasoning:

The term $3^5$ (243) is the only odd number in the group. All others have a factor of 2, making them even numbers. Also, it is the only one with a single distinct prime base.

Answer: $3^5$ does not belong.
