Problem 1: Area Under a Curve

Find the area under the curve of the function \( f(x) = x^2 \) from \( x = 0 \) to \( x = 10 \).

Figure 1: Graph of \( y = x^2 \)

Step-by-Step Explanation:

To find the area under the curve, we calculate the definite integral of the function:

$$ Area = \int_{0}^{10} x^2 \, dx $$

Using the power rule for integration \( \int x^n dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} \):

$$ = \left[ \frac{x^3}{3} \right]_{0}^{10} $$

Evaluate at the bounds:

$$ = \left( \frac{10^3}{3} \right) - \left( \frac{0^3}{3} \right) $$ $$ = \frac{1000}{3} \approx 333.33 $$

Final Answer: The area is approximately 333.33 square units.

Problem 2: Triangle Geometry

Given a right-angled triangle with a base of 30 units and a height of 40 units, calculate the length of the hypotenuse.

Figure 2: The Right Triangle

Step-by-Step Explanation:

We use the Pythagorean theorem:

$$ a^2 + b^2 = c^2 $$

Substitute the given values:

$$ 30^2 + 40^2 = c^2 $$ $$ 900 + 1600 = c^2 $$ $$ 2500 = c^2 $$ $$ c = \sqrt{2500} = 50 $$

Final Answer: The hypotenuse is 50 units.
