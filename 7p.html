Making Marriage Work

An interactive exploration of Dr. John Gottman's 7 Principles, designed to strengthen relationships, avoid the predictors of divorce, and build a meaningful future together.

The Four Horsemen

In his research, Dr. Gottman identified four negative communication styles that can predict the end of a relationship. By learning to identify these "Horsemen" and applying their antidotes, couples can resolve conflict productively.

1. Criticism

Attacking your partner's character or personality rather than focusing on the specific behavior.

Antidote: Gentle Start-up. Talk about your feelings using "I" statements and express a positive need.

2. Contempt

Assuming a position of moral superiority over your partner (sarcasm, cynicism, name-calling, eye-rolling).

Antidote: Build a Culture of Appreciation. Remind yourself of your partner's positive qualities.

3. Defensiveness

Self-protection in the form of righteous indignation or innocent victimhood to ward off a perceived attack.

Antidote: Take Responsibility. Accept your partner's perspective and offer an apology for any wrongdoing.

4. Stonewalling

Withdrawing from the interaction, shutting down, and simply stopping responding to your partner.

Antidote: Physiological Self-Soothing. Take a 20-minute break to calm down before returning to the conversation.

The 7 Principles (Flashcards)

Click on any card to flip it and read the summary of the principle.

1

Enhance Your Love Maps

Click to flip

A "love map" is the part of your brain where you store all the relevant information about your partner's life. Know their worries, hopes, goals, and history. Happy couples are intimately familiar with each other's worlds.

2

Nurture Fondness & Admiration

Click to flip

This is the antidote to contempt. It means maintaining a fundamental sense that your partner is worthy of being respected and liked. Remind yourself of their positive qualities and express your appreciation out loud.

3

Turn Toward Each Other

Click to flip

Partners constantly make "bids" for each other's attention, affection, or support. Turning toward these bids (rather than away or against) forms the basis of emotional connection, romance, and a healthy emotional bank account.

4

Let Your Partner Influence You

Click to flip

Happy couples are a team that considers each other's perspectives and feelings. Making decisions together and searching for common ground is crucial rather than exerting power over one another.

5

Solve Your Solvable Problems

Click to flip

Use a gentle startup, learn to make and receive repair attempts, soothe yourself and each other, compromise, and be tolerant of each other's faults when tackling situational conflicts.

6

Overcome Gridlock

Click to flip

For perpetual problems (the 69% of issues that never go away), the goal is not to solve them, but to move from gridlock to dialogue. Understand the underlying dreams and values fueling your partner's position.

7

Create Shared Meaning

Click to flip

Marriage isn't just about fighting less; it's about building a life together. Create rituals of connection, align your roles, support each other's life goals, and build a shared culture and legacy.
