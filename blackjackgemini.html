Basic Probability Strategy Guide

Use these rules to test if your decisions align with mathematical probability to maximize your chances over time. (Note: This simulation does not include splitting or doubling down).

Definitions

  • Hard Hand: A hand without an Ace, or where the Ace must count as 1 to avoid busting (e.g., 10 + 7, or A + 6 + 10).
  • Soft Hand: A hand containing an Ace counted as 11 (e.g., A + 6 is a "Soft 17"). It's impossible to bust a soft hand with one hit.

Player Strategy - Hard Hands

  • If you have 8 or less: Always Hit.
  • If you have 9 - 11: Always Hit (in real games, you'd often double down here).
  • If you have 12 - 16: This is the "Stiff Hand" danger zone.
    • Stand if the Dealer's face-up card is weak (2, 3, 4, 5, or 6). They have a high chance of busting.
    • Hit if the Dealer's face-up card is strong (7, 8, 9, 10, J, Q, K, A). You need to improve your hand to win.
  • If you have 17 or higher: Always Stand. The risk of busting is too high.

Player Strategy - Soft Hands (Contains an Ace = 11)

  • If you have Soft 13 - 15 (A+2 to A+4): Hit.
  • If you have Soft 16 - 17 (A+5 to A+6): Hit. There is no risk of busting, try to improve.
  • If you have Soft 18 (A+7): Stand if dealer shows 2-8. Hit if dealer shows 9, 10, A.
  • If you have Soft 19 or higher: Always Stand.
