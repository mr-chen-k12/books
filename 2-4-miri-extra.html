Set 1: Division Word Problems (Like #3)

1. A teacher distributed 35 pencils equally among 5 students. How many pencils did each student get?
A) 6
B) 7
C) 8
D) 5
Correct Answer: B) 7
Reasoning: Divide the total items by the number of groups: \( 35 \div 5 = 7 \).
2. There are 48 cookies in a jar. If you put them into bags of 6 cookies each, how many bags do you need?
A) 8
B) 9
C) 7
D) 6
Correct Answer: A) 8
Reasoning: \( 48 \div 6 = 8 \).
3. A gardener planted 24 flowers in 3 equal rows. How many flowers were in each row?
A) 6
B) 12
C) 9
D) 8
Correct Answer: D) 8
Reasoning: \( 24 \div 3 = 8 \).
4. The librarian stacked 56 books on 7 shelves. If each shelf has the same number of books, how many books are on one shelf?
A) 7
B) 9
C) 8
D) 6
Correct Answer: C) 8
Reasoning: \( 56 \div 7 = 8 \).
5. Four friends shared a pack of 32 stickers equally. How many stickers did each friend receive?
A) 8
B) 4
C) 7
D) 9
Correct Answer: A) 8
Reasoning: \( 32 \div 4 = 8 \).

Set 4: Counting Coins (Like #30)

16. You have exactly two coins: one Quarter and one Dime. What is the total amount?
A) $0.30
B) $0.35
C) $0.40
D) $0.45
Correct Answer: B) $0.35
Reasoning: Quarter ($0.25) + Dime ($0.10) = $0.35.
17. Which two coins add up to $0.75?
A) Two Quarters
B) A Half Dollar and a Quarter
C) A Half Dollar and a Dime
D) Three Quarters
Correct Answer: B) A Half Dollar and a Quarter
Reasoning: Half Dollar ($0.50) + Quarter ($0.25) = $0.75.
18. What is the total value of one Half Dollar and one Nickel?
A) $0.55
B) $0.60
C) $0.45
D) $0.15
Correct Answer: A) $0.55
Reasoning: Half Dollar ($0.50) + Nickel ($0.05) = $0.55.
19. Which amount can you make using exactly two coins?
A) $0.13
B) $0.26
C) $0.08
D) $0.29
Correct Answer: B) $0.26
Reasoning: A Quarter ($0.25) + A Penny ($0.01) = $0.26. The others require more than 2 coins.
20. What is the total value of two Dimes and one Penny?
A) $0.11
B) $0.21
C) $0.31
D) $0.25
Correct Answer: B) $0.21
Reasoning: 10 + 10 + 1 = 21 cents ($0.21).
