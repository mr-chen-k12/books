Problem 9: Number Line
To what number is the arrow pointing? Write the number two different ways.
The number line shows negative integers. The arrow is positioned exactly halfway between $-1$ and $-2$.
In decimals: halfway between $-1$ and $-2$ is $-1.5$.
In fractions: halfway is $-1 \frac{1}{2}$.
Answer: $-1.5$ and $-1 \frac{1}{2}$
Problem 10: Value of Coins
When T'Von emptied his bank, he found 17 pennies, 4 nickels, 5 dimes, and 2 quarters. What was the value of the coins?
Pennies: $17 \times \$0.01 = \$0.17$
Nickels: $4 \times \$0.05 = \$0.20$
Dimes: $5 \times \$0.10 = \$0.50$
Quarters: $2 \times \$0.25 = \$0.50$
Total: $\$0.17 + \$0.20 + \$0.50 + \$0.50 = \$1.37$
Answer: $1.37 (or 137 cents)

Problem 13: Decimal Chain Addition
$$ 87.6 + 4.0 + 31.7 + 5.5 + 1.1 + 0.5 = $$

$87.6 + 4.0 = 91.6$
$91.6 + 31.7 = 123.3$
$123.3 + 5.5 = 128.8$
$128.8 + 1.1 = 129.9$
$129.9 + 0.5 = 130.4$
Answer: 130.4

Problem 25: Estimation
The answer to 52 divided by 9 is not a whole number. What whole number represents a reasonable estimate? Explain.
Multiples of 9 close to 52 are 45 ($9 \times 5$) and 54 ($9 \times 6$).
52 is closer to 54 than to 45.
Since $54 \div 9 = 6$, 6 is the best estimate.
Answer: 6, because 9 x 6 is 54, which is close to 52.

Problem 30: Permutations
Show all the different ways these bills ($1, $5, $10) can be arranged in a row.
This is a permutation problem. We have 3 distinct items.
Possible arrangements:
  • $1, $5, $10
  • $1, $10, $5
  • $5, $1, $10
  • $5, $10, $1
  • $10, $1, $5
  • $10, $5, $1
Answer: There are 6 different ways.
