Lesson 78: Written Practice Solutions

Problem 1
What is the difference when the product of \(\frac{1}{2}\) and \(\frac{1}{2}\) is subtracted from the sum of \(\frac{1}{2}\) and \(\frac{1}{2}\)?

Step 1: Find the product.
Product = \(\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{4}\)

Step 2: Find the sum.
Sum = \(\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2} = \frac{2}{2} = 1\)

Step 3: Find the difference.
Subtract the product from the sum: \(1 - \frac{1}{4} = \frac{4}{4} - \frac{1}{4} = \mathbf{\frac{3}{4}}\)

Problem 2
The claws of a Siberian tiger are 10 centimeters long. How many millimeters is that?

We know that \(1 \text{ cm} = 10 \text{ mm}\).

\(10 \text{ cm} \times 10 \text{ mm/cm} = \mathbf{100 \text{ mm}}\)

Problem 3
Sue was thinking of a number between 40 and 50 that is a multiple of 3 and 4. Of what number was she thinking?

A number that is a multiple of both 3 and 4 must be a multiple of their Least Common Multiple (LCM).

LCM of 3 and 4 is 12.

Multiples of 12 are: 12, 24, 36, 48, 60...

The only multiple that falls between 40 and 50 is 48.

Problem 4
Four fifths of the 60 lights were on.
a. Into how many parts have the 60 lights been divided?
b. How many lights are in each part?
c. How many lights were on?
d. How many lights were off?

a. The denominator of the fraction \(\frac{4}{5}\) is 5, so the lights are divided into 5 parts.

b. \(60 \div 5 = \mathbf{12}\) lights in each part.

c. 4 parts were on. \(4 \times 12 = \mathbf{48}\) lights were on.

d. 1 part was off (5 parts total - 4 parts on). \(1 \times 12 = \mathbf{12}\) lights were off.

Problem 5
Which counting number is neither a prime number nor a composite number?

The number 1 is neither prime nor composite.

Problem 6
Find the unknown number: \(\frac{4}{5}m = 1\)

To solve for \(m\), multiply by the reciprocal of \(\frac{4}{5}\), which is \(\frac{5}{4}\).

\(m = 1 \times \frac{5}{4} = \frac{5}{4}\) or \(\mathbf{1\frac{1}{4}}\)

Problem 7
Find the unknown number: \(\frac{4}{5} + w = 1\)

Subtract \(\frac{4}{5}\) from 1.

\(w = 1 - \frac{4}{5} = \frac{5}{5} - \frac{4}{5} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{5}}\)

Problem 8
Find the unknown number: \(\frac{4}{5} \div x = 1\)

Any number divided by itself is 1.

Therefore, \(x = \mathbf{\frac{4}{5}}\)

Problem 9
Find the unknown number: \(\frac{3}{4} = \frac{n}{100}\)

To go from denominator 4 to 100, we multiply by 25.

We must do the same to the numerator: \(3 \times 25 = 75\).

\(n = \mathbf{75}\)

Problem 10

b. Write the answer to part a as a decimal number.

The rectangle is a grid of 2 rows and 5 columns, totaling 10 small squares.

Problem 12

a. \(\frac{3}{5} \bigcirc 0.35\)

Problem 13
\(\frac{5}{6} - \frac{1}{2}\)

Find a common denominator (6).

\(\frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{6}\)

\(\frac{5}{6} - \frac{3}{6} = \frac{2}{6}\)

Simplify: \(\mathbf{\frac{1}{3}}\)

Problem 14
\(4\frac{1}{4} - 3\frac{1}{3}\)

Find common denominator (12).

\(4\frac{3}{12} - 3\frac{4}{12}\)

We cannot subtract 4 from 3, so borrow from the whole number 4.

\(4\frac{3}{12} = 3\frac{15}{12}\) (Since \(1 = \frac{12}{12}\))

\(3\frac{15}{12} - 3\frac{4}{12} = \mathbf{\frac{11}{12}}\)

Problem 15
\(\frac{1}{2} + \frac{2}{3} + \frac{5}{6}\)

The Least Common Denominator is 6.

\(\frac{3}{6} + \frac{4}{6} + \frac{5}{6} = \frac{12}{6}\)

\(\frac{12}{6} = \mathbf{2}\)

Problem 16
\(1\frac{1}{2} \times 2\frac{2}{3}\)

Convert to improper fractions:

\(1\frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{2}\)

\(2\frac{2}{3} = \frac{8}{3}\)

Multiply: \(\frac{3}{2} \times \frac{8}{3}\)

Cancel out the 3s and simplify \(\frac{8}{2}\).

Answer: \(\mathbf{4}\)

Problem 17
\(1\frac{1}{2} \div 2\frac{2}{3}\)

Convert to improper fractions:

\(\frac{3}{2} \div \frac{8}{3}\)

Multiply by the reciprocal of the divisor:

\(\frac{3}{2} \times \frac{3}{8} = \mathbf{\frac{9}{16}}\)

Problem 18
\(2\frac{2}{3} \div 1\frac{1}{2}\)

Convert to improper fractions:

\(\frac{8}{3} \div \frac{3}{2}\)

Multiply by reciprocal:

\(\frac{8}{3} \times \frac{2}{3} = \frac{16}{9}\)

Convert to mixed number: \(\mathbf{1\frac{7}{9}}\)

Problem 19

Problem 20
"The opposite sides of a rectangle are parallel." True or false?

True. By definition, a rectangle is a parallelogram, which means its opposite sides are parallel.

Problem 21
What is the average of \(3^3\) and \(5^2\)?

First, evaluate the exponents:

\(3^3 = 3 \times 3 \times 3 = 27\)

\(5^2 = 5 \times 5 = 25\)

Find the average:

\(\frac{27 + 25}{2} = \frac{52}{2} = \mathbf{26}\)

Problem 22
The diameter of the small wheel was 7 inches. The circumference was about 22 inches. Write the ratio of the circumference to the diameter of the circle as a decimal number rounded to the nearest hundredth.

Ratio = \(\frac{\text{Circumference}}{\text{Diameter}} = \frac{22}{7}\)

Perform the division: \(22 \div 7 \approx 3.1428...\)

Rounded to the nearest hundredth: \(\mathbf{3.14}\)

Problem 26
Find the prime factorization of 900 by using a factor tree. Then write the prime factorization using exponents.

Factor Tree Steps:

  • 900 = 90 × 10
  • 90 = 9 × 10
  • 9 = 3 × 3 (Prime)
  • 10 = 2 × 5 (Prime)
  • Original 10 = 2 × 5 (Prime)

Factors: \(3, 3, 2, 5, 2, 5\)

Grouped: \(2 \times 2 \times 3 \times 3 \times 5 \times 5\)

Using exponents: \(\mathbf{2^2 \cdot 3^2 \cdot 5^2}\)

Problem 27
Three vertices of a rectangle have the coordinates (5, 3), (5, -1), and (-1, -1). What are the coordinates of the fourth vertex of the rectangle?

Plot the points mentally:

  • (5, -1) is the bottom-right corner.
  • (5, 3) is the top-right corner.
  • (-1, -1) is the bottom-left corner.

To complete the rectangle, we need the top-left corner.

It must have the x-coordinate of the left side (-1) and the y-coordinate of the top side (3).

Answer: \(\mathbf{(-1, 3)}\)

Problem 28
Refer to this table to answer a and b.
3 teaspoons = 1 tablespoon
16 tablespoons = 1 cup
2 cups = 1 pint
2 pints = 1 quart
4 quarts = 1 gallon

a. A teaspoon of soup is what fraction of a tablespoon of soup?
b. How many cups of milk is a gallon of milk?

a. Since 3 teaspoons make 1 tablespoon, 1 teaspoon is \(\mathbf{\frac{1}{3}}\) of a tablespoon.

b. Work upwards from gallons to cups:

  • 1 gallon = 4 quarts
  • 4 quarts = \(4 \times 2 = 8\) pints
  • 8 pints = \(8 \times 2 = 16\) cups

Answer: \(\mathbf{16 \text{ cups}}\)

Problem 29
A liter is closest in size to which of the following?
A. pint    B. quart    C. 1/2 gallon    D. gallon

A liter is approximately equal to 1.057 quarts.

Therefore, it is closest to a B. quart.

Problem 30
In 1881 Clara Barton founded the American Red Cross... How many ounces is a pint?

In the US customary system:

1 pint = 2 cups

1 cup = 8 fluid ounces

\(2 \times 8 = 16\)

Answer: \(\mathbf{16 \text{ ounces}}\)
