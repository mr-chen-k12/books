Problem 1
Evita had 56 beads that she was putting into bags. She wanted to put them into equal groups of 8 beads. How many bags will she need?

Steps: Divide the total number of beads by the number of beads per group.

$$ 56 \div 8 = 7 $$

Answer: 7 bags

Problem 2
There were 42 children waiting for a ride. There were 7 cars available. If the same number of children rode each car, then how many would be in each car?

Steps: Divide the total children by the number of cars.

$$ 42 \div 7 = 6 $$

Answer: 6 children

Problem 3
Write four multiplication/division facts using the numbers 4, 7, and 28.

Fact Family:

  • \( 4 \times 7 = 28 \)
  • \( 7 \times 4 = 28 \)
  • \( 28 \div 4 = 7 \)
  • \( 28 \div 7 = 4 \)
Problem 4
Which months have exactly 30 days?

Explanation: Recall the rhyme "Thirty days hath September, April, June, and November..."

Answer: April, June, September, November

Problem 5
Consider this sequence: ..., 16,000, 17,000, 18,000, 19,000, ...
a. Write a rule that describes how to find the next term.
b. What is the next term of the sequence?

a. Rule: The numbers are increasing by one thousand each time.

Rule: Add 1,000

b. Next Term: \( 19,000 + 1,000 = 20,000 \)

Answer: 20,000

Problem 6
a. Round 4728 to the nearest hundred.
b. Round 4728 to the nearest ten.

a. Nearest Hundred: Look at the tens digit (2). Since 2 < 5, round down.

Answer: 4,700

b. Nearest Ten: Look at the ones digit (8). Since 8 > 5, round up.

Answer: 4,730

Problem 7
Write the time "a quarter after four in the afternoon" in digital form.

Explanation: "Quarter after" means 15 minutes past the hour. "Afternoon" means PM.

Answer: 4:15 PM

Problem 8
One side of a square is 4 feet long.
a. What is the perimeter?
b. What is the area?

a. Perimeter: Add all sides ($4+4+4+4$).

\( P = 16 \) feet

b. Area: Multiply length $\times$ width ($4 \times 4$).

\( A = 16 \) square feet (or sq ft)

Answer: \( 4 \frac{1}{8} \)

Problem 10
Describe the order of operations and find the number it equals: \( \sqrt{64} + (42 \div 6) \)

Steps:

  1. Solve the square root: \( \sqrt{64} = 8 \)
  2. Solve inside parenthesis: \( 42 \div 6 = 7 \)
  3. Add the results: \( 8 + 7 = 15 \)

Answer: 15

Problem 26

  • D: \( 6 \times 0 = 0 \) (Not 6)

Problem 27
a. A quarter is what percent of a dollar?
b. A quart is what percent of a gallon?

a. A quarter is 25 cents. A dollar is 100 cents. \( \frac{25}{100} = 25\% \)

b. There are 4 quarts in a gallon. 1 quart is \( \frac{1}{4} \). \( \frac{1}{4} = 25\% \)

Answer: a) 25%, b) 25%

Problem 29
Brandon purchased 1 liter of juice, which is about 67.6 fluid ounces. Estimate the number of cups of juice that Brandon purchased. Explain your thinking.

Reasoning: We know 1 cup is approx 8 fluid ounces.

To estimate, we divide 67.6 by 8.

\( 8 \times 8 = 64 \), which is close to 67.6.

Answer: About 8.5 cups (or between 8 and 9 cups)

Problem 30
The 900 North Michigan Avenue building is 871 feet tall. The 181 West Madison Street building is 680 feet tall. How many feet taller is the 900 North Michigan Avenue building?

Subtract the smaller height from the larger height:

\( 871 - 680 = 191 \)

Answer: 191 feet
